## Persona 1 — stats_methods

### Review

**Summary assessment.** The paper's statistical spine is, on its current terms, unusually clean for a humanistic-corpus submission. The authors use a `sklearn.pipeline.Pipeline` to wrap the `StandardScaler` with the `LogisticRegression` estimator so that scaling statistics are refit within each cross-validation fold; a pre-release scaler-leakage error is disclosed in Appendix B.1 and the corrected numbers are the only ones cited in §5. Three complementary tests (classical ANOVA, Welch's robust ANOVA, Kruskal–Wallis) are reported for all sixteen dimensions, with $\eta^2$ effect sizes alongside capped p-values. A 1000-shuffle label-permutation test is reported for the headline classifier. Wilson 95% confidence intervals appear on every recall estimate and on every classifier accuracy. This is how a methods reviewer wants to be met.

**Kill-criteria audit.**

- *Multiple-comparisons correction absent?* — **Not triggered, but imperfectly handled.** Sixteen ANOVAs are reported (Table 1) without an explicit Bonferroni or Benjamini–Hochberg adjustment. The authors justify this by capping p-values at $10^{-15}$, noting that scipy's $F$/$H$ distributions underflow before that bound. At that magnitude, a sixteen-way Bonferroni correction (multiplier 16) leaves the raw p-values trivially below any conventional $\alpha$. I would still like the paper to say this out loud: "All sixteen tests survive a Bonferroni correction at the reported $p$-floor." It is a two-sentence addition, and its absence reads as methodological innocence rather than as an actual statistical failure.
- *Null-model design tests what it claims?* — **Not triggered.** The permutation test shuffles section labels, refits the classifier under the same stratified 10-fold protocol, and compares the observed accuracy to the null distribution. This is Ojala & Garriga 2010–standard; it tests the correct null (label-independence under the full pipeline) rather than the easier-to-fake "permute coordinates" null. The reported $p_{\text{perm}} = 9.99 \times 10^{-4}$ is the floor of a 1000-shuffle test, correctly reported as such.
- *Effect sizes reported?* — **Not triggered.** $\eta^2$ is reported for every dimension, Wilson CIs for every recall, and classifier accuracies all carry Wilson intervals. The paper is more effect-size-conscious than most CS submissions I see.
- *Data leakage in preprocessing?* — **Not triggered.** The pipeline is correct, the prior error is disclosed, and this level of candour is rare enough to note approvingly.
- *Torsion / curvature estimation sensitive to binning without robustness analysis?* — **N/A.** No such estimate appears in this paper.

**Evaluation-rubric answers.**

- *Is the null distribution appropriate for what the paper claims to test?* — Yes. Label-shuffling within the same Pipeline-wrapped protocol tests "does the pipeline find section structure when there is none?", which is the claim.
- *Multiple-comparisons correction survivability?* — Yes, but should be stated.
- *Effect sizes?* — Yes.
- *Confidence intervals on primary estimates?* — Yes, Wilson intervals throughout.
- *Preprocessing fit on held-out data?* — No, and the prior error is disclosed.
- *Sample-size / effect-size / power statement?* — Partially. The astronomical class ($n=12$) is explicitly flagged in §5.3 and §6.6 as having a wide CI despite a perfect point estimate. A formal power analysis would be overkill here but a short sentence in §4.2 on the floor $n$ at which classification stabilises would strengthen the paper.

**Intellectual-commitment-informed comments.**

- *p-values as diagnostic.* The authors treat the $<10^{-15}$ p-values correctly — they do not celebrate them; they note the floor and pivot to $\eta^2$. This is the ASA-statement-aligned posture.
- *Permutation inference preferred.* The headline significance test is permutation-based, not parametric, which is the right call for a $n=197$ corpus with imbalanced classes.
- *Multiple testing.* Flagged above. The fix is one sentence.
- *Bootstrap CIs.* Wilson is the right choice for proportion estimates; for the classifier accuracy itself, a bootstrap over the test-fold assignments would be a stronger complement than Wilson alone, but this is a "ceiling, not floor" ask and I would not block on it.

**DSH-specific concerns from this reviewer's methodological vantage.** My domain is statistical methods, not digital humanities. The DSH dossier flags several humanistic-framing hot-buttons I am not the right reviewer to assess: Drucker-adjacent reflection, distant-viewing lineage citation (Impett, Arnold & Tilton, Manovich), training-data provenance for the foundation model. What I *can* see from a methods vantage is that the manuscript opens with a method-led framing ("first systematic computational visual semantic analysis"), not a DH-question-led framing, and that §2 positions the work against text-side decipherment attempts rather than against distant-viewing / cultural-analytics tradition. From a CS-venue perspective this is idiomatic; from the DSH scope-exclusion trigger ("AI/data science research ... unless it has direct relevance to Digital Humanities") it reads as a framing risk. That is a framing observation, not a methods objection.

I also note that §3.2 withholds the foundation model's identity, the dimension-descriptor text, and the normalisation/temperature details under a pending patent. From a methods standpoint this is a reproducibility gap — Appendix B.1 addresses it honestly and commits to statistical-layer reproducibility via the released profile vectors, which is the right minimum — but a methods reviewer at *any* venue would prefer full disclosure. At DSH the humanistic-epistemic frame will compound this ask with an additional training-data-provenance requirement. I flag the tension: the current withholding is Axiom-III-honest ("we say so") but will draw reviewer pushback; the fix is not to soften the claim, it is to expand §3.2 with as much provenance as the patent posture allows (model family, training-corpus class, known bias characteristics of that corpus class).

**Specific revision requests from this reviewer.**

1. One-sentence note in §5.2 that the sixteen ANOVAs survive Bonferroni correction at the reported p-floor.
2. Short power/sample-size note in §4.2 acknowledging that astronomical ($n=12$) and pharmaceutical ($n=20$) classes carry the most uncertainty and that the classifier numbers for those two classes should be read with their Wilson intervals, not the point estimates.
3. Optional but recommended: bootstrap confidence interval on the LOOCV accuracy as a complement to the Wilson interval.
4. In §3.2, expand the patent-withholding discussion to include whatever provenance can be disclosed about the foundation model's training-corpus class, even if the specific model is not named. This is a methods-reviewer ask that happens to align with the DSH training-data-bias hot-button.

**Verdict rationale.** No kill-criteria triggered. The statistics are clean. The only methods-side concerns are minor and fixable without touching a single numerical claim. The framing risk at DSH is real but is a framing issue, not a statistical one, and I am not the reviewer who should be deciding it.

## Persona 2 — manifold_learning_skeptic

### Review

**Summary assessment.** The paper reports UMAP and PCA projections (§5.5), a lens-specificity control (§5.4), an out-of-distribution probe (§5.9), and a raw-embedding ablation (§5.3.1). My signature concerns — null-data controls, hyperparameter sensitivity, principal-curve goodness-of-fit, density confounds, matched-capacity baselines — are partially addressed. There is one serious gap.

**Kill-criteria audit.**

- *Claim about manifold structure without null-data control at matched dimensionality?* — **Partially triggered.** §5.5 reports that "the five sections separate spontaneously" in UMAP axes 1×2, 2×3, and a linear PCA sanity check. The PCA panel is the right kind of control for a nonlinear-DR artefact objection — if the structure appears in the deterministic linear projection, UMAP is not inventing it — and I credit the authors for including it. What is missing is the **Chari-&-Pachter-style null**: run the exact same UMAP and PCA pipeline on data with matched shape and marginal statistics but no real structure (isotropic 16-d Gaussian, covariance-matched Gaussian drawn from the pooled per-section covariance, or dim-shuffled within-page). The claim in §5.5 ("the dimensionality-reduction step sees only the 16-d profile vectors and is never told the section labels" and then separates them) is a real observation but it is not protected against the UMAP-imposes-shapes-on-anything objection. The linear PCA panel helps; a null-data control would close the objection cleanly. As written, the claim survives by the skin of PCA's teeth.
- *UMAP hyperparameter sweep absent?* — **Partially triggered.** §5.5 reports a 10-seed Procrustes-RMSE stability analysis (Figure 9) showing that cluster identity is stable across random seeds but that cluster orientation is not. This addresses *initialisation* sensitivity (the Kobak & Linderman 2021 concern) directly. What is not reported is the (n_neighbors, min_dist) hyperparameter grid. The paper reports one UMAP setting. I do not think the section structure is going to disappear at another setting — the PCA panel makes that clear — but I would like to see a small grid, or an explicit statement that the claim rests primarily on the deterministic PCA and only illustratively on UMAP.
- *Principal-curve goodness-of-fit not reported?* — **Not triggered.** The paper does not fit principal curves; it reports cluster separations and classifier accuracy. The herbal-cluster observation in §5.5 — "the 118 herbal pages do not form a single blob but a visible elongated manifold" — is a *qualitative* observation not advanced as a principal-curve claim, and the authors appropriately decline to interpret it further, deferring a Currier-hand correlation analysis to future work. The right call.
- *Matched-capacity baseline missing?* — **Not triggered.** The raw 768-d embedding ablation (§5.3.1) is exactly the matched-capacity baseline I would ask for: it tells the reader what classification accuracy the foundation model provides *without* the 16-d projection, which answers the "is the archetype lens doing the work or is the VLM doing the work?" question directly. The honest finding that the raw embedding slightly outperforms the 16-d projection (92.4% vs 90.4%) is exactly the kind of result an author with a methodological conscience reports. I am favourably disposed to this paper on that basis alone.
- *Density confounds not addressed?* — **Minor trigger.** UMAP's density-dependent distortions mean that the "pharmaceutical cluster sits inside the herbal cluster" observation in §5.5 is partly a density artefact — pharmaceutical has 20 pages, herbal has 118, and UMAP will embed the smaller cluster into the envelope of the larger one. The authors' classifier confusion matrix (Figure 6) tells the same story from a non-DR angle, which is reassuring, but a one-sentence note that the UMAP lobe-inside-cluster geometry is consistent with both a semantic-proximity reading and a density-distortion reading would be honest.

**Evaluation-rubric answers.**

- *Does the pipeline produce its finding on null data of matched shape?* — Not reported. This is my primary ask.
- *Is the UMAP (n_neighbors, min_dist) sweep reported?* — No, only the 10-seed stability analysis.
- *Principal-curve GOF?* — Not advanced as a claim, correctly.
- *Does the paper distinguish projection-head effects from simple dimension reduction?* — **Yes, and unusually well.** §5.3.1 reports both the 16-d archetype projection and the raw 768-d embedding side-by-side. This is the projection-head comparison that most papers omit.
- *Alternative DR methods shown?* — PCA is shown as a sanity check. t-SNE or PaCMAP would be a nice additional redundancy check but I do not require it given PCA is present.

**Intellectual-commitment-informed comments.** My three strong positions are (a) UMAP can impose arbitrary shapes on null data, (b) principal curves fit any cloud, (c) initialisation and hyperparameters shape global structure. The paper addresses (c) well via the Procrustes-RMSE analysis, correctly avoids (b) by not making principal-curve claims, and partially addresses (a) via the PCA sanity check. The missing piece is a matched-marginal null-data run through the full pipeline. This is not a kill-level objection because the classifier result (§5.3) is the primary load-bearing finding and it is protected by the permutation test, which tests the correct null. The UMAP is supporting evidence, not the main claim — and it is presented as such.

**DSH-specific concerns from this reviewer's methodological vantage.** My domain is nonlinear dimension reduction as used in single-cell genomics and representation learning; it is not digital humanities. The DSH dossier's concerns about Drucker, distant-viewing lineage, and training-data provenance are outside my expertise and I will not pretend otherwise. What I *can* see is that the paper's reflection on "what UMAP shows versus what a linear projection shows" (§5.5) is the kind of methodological self-awareness that DSH reviewers reportedly reward — it is epistemologically accountable in the sense the dossier describes. The linear PCA sanity check could profitably be framed in the paper not as "a deterministic fallback against UMAP's non-determinism" (CS-framing) but as "a second method with different assumptions that agrees with the first" (DH-framing). This is a framing observation, not a technical one.

**Specific revision requests from this reviewer.**

1. **Add a matched-marginal null-data run** through the full profile-→-UMAP-→-PCA-→-classifier pipeline. Draw 197 samples from a 16-d isotropic Gaussian with matched per-dimension variance, assign them random section labels stratified to match the true class sizes, and rerun §5.3–§5.5. Report that (a) the classifier accuracy collapses to chance-plus-class-imbalance (~35%), and (b) the UMAP on null data does not produce the section-separated geometry seen in Figure 4. This is a one-afternoon addition; it closes the "UMAP shape artefact" objection at the epistemic root.
2. Add a small UMAP hyperparameter grid, or an explicit statement that the UMAP is illustrative and the load-bearing DR is the deterministic PCA panel.
3. One-sentence acknowledgement that the pharmaceutical-inside-herbal UMAP lobe is consistent with both a semantic-proximity reading and a density-distortion reading. The classifier confusion matrix disambiguates in favour of the former; say that.

**Verdict rationale.** No kill-criterion is triggered hard enough to reject. The null-data control is the most important missing piece, but the paper's headline claim rests on classifier accuracy, not on UMAP geometry, and the classifier claim is protected by the permutation test and the raw-embedding ablation. The missing pieces are fixable without altering any numerical claim. I would revise rather than reject, and I would do so at the "please add these three things and resubmit" level rather than the "fundamental methodological concern" level.

## Persona 3 — probing_methodology

### Review

**Summary assessment.** I am reading this paper as a probing-methodology reviewer — my canonical concern is that a probe's output reflects the joint capacity of the probe and the model, not the model alone, and that probe results presented as model-properties require probe-capacity calibration and a control-task null (Hewitt & Liang 2019). The visual-semantic-profiling pipeline described in §3 is functionally a cross-modal linear probe: a human-authored natural-language descriptor is encoded by the text side of a frozen VLM, each page image is encoded by the vision side, and the probe output is the cosine similarity. The 16-d profile is therefore a probe readout; the multinomial-logistic classifier on the profile is a second-order probe. This paper has probe-on-top-of-probe structure, which is exactly the structure my methodology is built to interrogate.

**Kill-criteria audit.**

- *Probe result presented as model-property without probe-capacity calibration?* — **Partially triggered but mitigated.** The paper does *not* make a model-property claim; it makes a manuscript-property claim ("the illustrations encode thematic structure accessible to computational analysis"). That is the right framing for this kind of evidence. What is missing is an explicit probe-capacity control in the Hewitt & Liang sense. The closest artefact is the lens-specificity control in §5.4, which reports that two unrelated 16-d lenses (archaeology, cryptological) also recover section structure at 84.8% and 87.3%. This is *almost* a control-task null — it tests whether the finding is specific to the authored lens — but it is not *quite* the Hewitt & Liang standard, because all three lenses are semantically-meaningful rather than random. A true control-task null in my tradition would be a lens of sixteen *random* natural-language strings (word-lists sampled from an unrelated corpus, or strings drawn from a nonsense-string generator), processed through the same pipeline. If the random lens also recovers section structure at 80%, the section signal lives in the raw visual embedding and the archetype lens is epiphenomenal. If the random lens drops to chance while the semantically-meaningful lenses all cluster around 85–90%, the archetype lens is doing real work.
- *No control-task / random-mapping null reported?* — **Triggered.** This is the core missing piece. The lens-specificity control (§5.4) is three semantically-meaningful lenses; it is not a random-mapping null. The raw-embedding ablation (§5.3.1) is a matched-capacity control on the visual side, which is excellent — but it is not a random-lens control on the text/descriptor side.
- *Cross-modal cosine similarity without addressing modality-gap artefact risk?* — **Triggered as a minor.** The foundation model used is a VLM with a shared image-text embedding space; cosine similarities between image embeddings and text-descriptor embeddings are subject to the Liang et al. 2022 modality gap (embeddings from the two modalities occupy disjoint cones in the shared space, so cosine similarities are systematically compressed and the rank-ordering can differ from what a single-modality geometry would predict). The paper does not discuss this. For the manuscript's claim — that sections are *discriminable* from 16-d profiles — the modality gap is not fatal, because discriminability only requires that the gap-induced bias be approximately constant across dimensions, which is empirically the case in CLIP-style models. But the paper should acknowledge the modality gap exists and argue (or measure) that it does not confound the section-discrimination claim.
- *Researcher-degrees-of-freedom in probe construction unacknowledged?* — **Partially triggered but mitigated by Axiom-III-style candour.** §3.1 and §6.6 both state clearly that the dimension descriptors are human-authored, that the Voynich was among the motivating documents when they were written, that the honest framing is "supervised dimension design, zero-shot profile computation," and that no section label entered the design. §5.4 is presented as the empirical answer to "did the lens decide the result?" and I credit this — it is unusually honest. What is still missing is the *number* of revisions the sixteen dimensions went through before being frozen, and whether any dimension was dropped, renamed, or re-scoped in response to early Voynich profiles. Appendix B.1 discloses a prior scaler-leakage error, which is good; a similar disclosure on dimension-selection history would complete the forking-paths story.

**Evaluation-rubric answers.**

- *What is the probe, and what is its capacity?* — The probe is (text-encoder of frozen VLM) composed with (cosine similarity against image embedding). The capacity is the intersection of the VLM's text-encoder expressivity and the sixteen-string design space chosen by the authors. This is not explicitly stated; I would like it to be.
- *Control-task / random-mapping null?* — Missing. See kill-criteria.
- *Does the paper distinguish what the model knows from what the probe produces?* — Yes, §5.3.1 separates the raw 768-d VLM embedding from the 16-d archetype projection and reports both. This is the right comparison.
- *Probe revisions / silent pruning?* — Not disclosed; would like to see it.
- *Modality-gap addressed?* — Not addressed. Minor ask.

**Intellectual-commitment-informed comments.** My position is that probes are measurement instruments, calibrated first, then used. This paper uses a probe without calibrating it against a random-lens baseline, and the lens-specificity control (§5.4) is a laudable partial substitute rather than a full calibration. The honest version of this paper would report four lenses: voynich (90.4%), cryptological (87.3%), archaeology (84.8%), **random-string lens (???)**. I do not know what the fourth number is, but I know it is the number the paper is missing. If it is near 85% the archetype-lens claim weakens substantially — the signal lives in the visual embedding and the lens is almost epiphenomenal. If it drops to 50–60%, the archetype lens is doing meaningful work and the three semantically-meaningful lenses cluster at the high end because they are all *good* lenses in a way a random lens is not. Either outcome is publishable; not reporting the number is the problem.

**DSH-specific concerns from this reviewer's methodological vantage.** My domain is NLP interpretability and probing methodology. The DSH dossier asks for reflection on training-data provenance for the foundation model, and this is a question my methodology is natively equipped to answer even though my subfield is not DH. A probe can only surface structure that the underlying model's training data prepared it to see. The paper's foundation model is undisclosed (patent posture, §3.2), and without the disclosure the reader cannot evaluate whether the "herbal/botanical" dimension is firing because the model was trained on botanical imagery (likely) or because the Voynich plants are unusually recognisable (also possible). A training-data-class disclosure — "the model was trained on [class of] internet-scale image-text pairs, which is known to contain [class of] botanical and astronomical imagery" — would close this gap without compromising the patent. This is both a probing-methodology ask and a DSH hot-button ask; they align.

I also flag, from my vantage, that §2's engagement with prior art is text-decipherment-history-heavy and distant-viewing-lineage-light. The paper does not cite Arnold & Tilton's *Distant Viewing* (2023), Impett's computational art history, Manovich's *Cultural Analytics*, or Drucker's *Graphesis*. For a CV venue this is unusual but not fatal; for DSH the dossier flags this as a scope-exclusion trigger. I am not the right reviewer to enforce the DH-citation-bar — my bar is the probing-methodology bar — but I see enough of the DSH frame to note that this paper is one revision away from clearing it.

**Specific revision requests from this reviewer.**

1. **Add a random-lens control** to §5.4. Sixteen natural-language strings sampled from an unrelated corpus (Wikipedia random-article titles, Brown corpus first-sentences, NLTK word-list sixteen-grams — anything that produces syntactically-valid English strings with no semantic relation to the manuscript), run through the identical pipeline. Report the classifier accuracy. This is the Hewitt & Liang 2019 control-task standard.
2. **Disclose the dimension-design revision history.** How many versions of the sixteen dimensions were tried before the current set was frozen? What changed between versions? Was any dimension dropped in response to early profile-vector inspection? This is the forking-paths disclosure that completes the Axiom-III-style candour of §3.1 and Appendix B.1.
3. **Address the modality gap.** One paragraph, probably in §3.1 or §6.6: acknowledge the Liang et al. 2022 modality gap, state whether the pipeline's cosine-similarity comparisons are between same-modality embeddings (which would eliminate the concern) or across modalities (which would require the argument that the gap is approximately constant across dimensions and does not confound section-discrimination).
4. **Disclose training-corpus class of the foundation model**, even if the specific model remains behind the patent. "Internet-scale image-text pairs" vs "curated cultural-heritage corpus" vs "medical-imaging corpus" makes a first-order difference to how a probing reviewer reads the herbal/botanical dimension's performance, and it satisfies the DSH training-data-bias ask simultaneously.

**Verdict rationale.** The missing control-task null is a major kill-criterion in my tradition, but it is fixable by running the random-lens baseline — the code is already written for the three-lens control in §5.4 and only the lens-YAML changes. The paper's candour in §3.1, §5.3.1, §6.6, and Appendix B.1 convinces me that the authors will add what is asked and will report the number honestly regardless of how it lands. This is a REVISE, not a REJECT — but the revise is substantive and must be performed before acceptance.
