## Persona 1 — stats_methods

### Review

**Summary assessment.** The paper reports a vision-language classifier on n=197 pages of Beinecke MS 408, profiled through a 16-d archetype lens, evaluated against a five-section scholarly taxonomy under LOOCV and stratified 10-fold. Headline: 90.36% LOOCV accuracy with Wilson 95% CI [85.4%, 93.7%], permutation p ≈ 9.99e-4 on 1000 shuffles. From a statistical-methods vantage this is, candidly, an unusually well-behaved results section for a humanities-adjacent submission. The authors use a `Pipeline`-wrapped scaler-plus-LR (preventing the classical scaler-leakage bug, which they flag explicitly in B.1 — I appreciate both the correction and the transparent retraction of the pre-release number), Wilson score intervals on every per-class recall, permutation inference as the primary non-parametric test, and both parametric and non-parametric ANOVA with Welch correction where class imbalance warrants it. The effect-size reporting (η² per dimension) is exactly what I ask for and almost never get.

**Kill criteria — none triggered.** I walked my full list:

1. *Multiple-comparisons correction.* Sixteen ANOVA tests are reported. All p-values bottom out at the stated numerical floor (p < 1e-15) on all three test families, and η² is between 0.30 and 0.83. Even with a conservative Bonferroni across 16 × 3 = 48 comparisons the finding survives trivially (adjusted threshold 0.05/48 ≈ 1e-3; observed ≪ 1e-15). Authors could make the correction explicit — a one-line footnote saying "Bonferroni-adjusted across 48 tests leaves every dimension significant at α=0.05" would close the box — but the finding does not depend on this.
2. *Null-model design.* The permutation test shuffles **labels** under a held-fixed classifier and sample. This is the correct null (Ojala & Garriga 2010): it tests whether the feature-to-label mapping carries information beyond class marginals. I specifically checked this because the OOD probe in §5.9 uses a different pipeline and I was worried about which null is running where. The primary permutation null is label-permutation. Correct.
3. *Effect sizes alongside p-values.* η² reported per dimension. Wilson CIs reported on every recall. Bootstrap CIs on the cross-corpus similarity centroids would be nice-to-have but are not load-bearing.
4. *Data leakage.* The B.1 note is the cleanest leakage-audit disclosure I have seen in a submission. `Pipeline` wraps `StandardScaler` + `LogisticRegression`, `cross_val_predict` fits inside-fold. No leakage. The fact that they caught the pre-release bug themselves and document the delta is a positive signal about the analysis discipline.
5. *Power / sample size.* Flagged honestly for astronomical (n=12, recall point estimate 100%, CI [75.8%, 100%]). I would add one power note for the pharmaceutical class (n=20, 50% recall CI [29.9%, 70.1%]) — the CI is wide enough that the "pharmaceutical confuses with herbal" interpretation needs a caveat that with n=20 the classifier could be performing anywhere from "barely above chance" to "competent but noisy," and the confusion-matrix interpretation assumes the latter.

**Evaluation-rubric pass.** Null appropriate (yes). Correction applied implicitly (yes, would like explicit). Effect sizes reported (yes). CIs reported (yes). Leakage audited (yes, and documented). Sample-size/effect-size relationship stated (mostly; astronomical is flagged, pharmaceutical could use an additional caveat).

**Intellectual-commitment comments.** The authors treat p-values as diagnostic rather than conclusion — §5.2 ("p-values have all bottomed out on numerical noise, which on n=197 they have") is exactly the framing I advocate. The permutation inference is preferred over parametric in the headline, which is the methodologically defensible choice on a small imbalanced corpus. Bootstrap CIs would strengthen the cross-section centroid distances in §5.6 but this is a nice-to-have, not a kill.

**JOCCH-specific concerns.** JOCCH's ablation bar is the central methodological expectation at this venue, so I looked hard for whether the required baselines are *present and framed as baselines*. They are present: raw 768-d CLIP embedding ablation (§5.3.1, 92.4%), handcrafted 6-d layout ablation (§5.3.2, 72.1%), lens-specificity control with two non-target lenses (§5.4, 87.3% and 84.8%), text-channel char-n-gram head-to-head on the 182-page intersection (§5.10, 92.3% text vs 92.3% 16-d visual vs 96.2% raw 768-d), and an OOD probe across three comparison corpora (§5.9). This is the exact evidentiary shape JOCCH rewards. The framing, however, is slightly off: the ablations are introduced as subsections of §5.3 ("Ablation:…") rather than as first-class baselines in a baseline-comparison table. JOCCH reviewers will read this and want it restructured so that Table in §5.3.3 — which already compiles the five feature-space comparison — is promoted to a §5 top-level baseline section, with the headline 90.4% contextualised against all four competitors up front. This is framing, not evidence; it is a revision ask.

**Specific revision requests.**

- Add an explicit multiple-comparisons-correction sentence in §5.2 naming the method (Bonferroni or BH-FDR) and stating the adjusted threshold. The finding survives either, but the absence of an explicit statement will draw a reviewer comment at JOCCH.
- Add a power / CI-width caveat for the pharmaceutical recall result. The "honest misclassification" narrative is correct but depends on n=20 being enough to distinguish 50% recall from chance, which it barely is.
- Promote §5.3.3 to a first-class baseline comparison section. Reframe the 16-d lens as "one instrument among several" (the JOCCH dossier's exact phrasing) rather than as the headline with ablations underneath. Substance unchanged; ordering changed.
- Add bootstrap CIs to the §5.6 cross-section centroid distances. The range is narrow (>0.93 mean similarity) and a reviewer will want to see whether the ordering among neighbour ranks is stable.
- The six-section analysis (§5.7, 89.85% LOOCV) should report its permutation p-value too, not only its point estimate. Otherwise a reviewer will ask whether the five-section number is p-optimised against the six-section alternative.

**Verdict rationale.** No kill criterion triggered. The statistical methodology is at or above the JOCCH bar. The paper's framing — ablations as ancillary rather than as baselines — is fixable in a revision and is the dominant reason I cannot accept outright. The substantive claims (discrimination at p<1e-15 across 16 dims; 90.4% LOOCV against 59.9% majority baseline; pharmaceutical-herbal confusion matching scholarly structure; ablations ordered raw>lens>layout>majority) all survive my review.

## Persona 2 — manifold_learning_skeptic

### Review

**Summary assessment.** I read this paper with the UMAP figure (Figure 4) flagged as the single most methodologically load-bearing visual in the submission, and I read §5.5 with my Chari-and-Pachter hat on. I will say up front what I expected to find and did not: I expected to find a paper that leaned on a UMAP plot as its primary evidence for cluster structure, with no seed ensemble, no null-data control, no GOF-on-principal-curve analysis, and a headline claim about "manifold structure in the Voynich embeddings." This paper does not do that. The UMAP is demoted to §5.5 as a visualisation, the quantitative claims are carried by §5.2 (per-dim ANOVA) and §5.3 (supervised classifier LOOCV), a 10-seed Procrustes UMAP stability analysis is reported in Figure 9, and a deterministic linear PCA is shown side-by-side in Figure 4c as a sanity check against UMAP-inventing-the-clustering. This is the exact defensive posture I recommend to any author using UMAP on a small-to-medium dataset. The kill criteria in my standard checklist do not, mostly, fire here.

**Kill criteria — walkthrough.**

1. *Claim about manifold structure without null-data control at matched dimensionality.* The paper does not make a manifold-structure claim. §5.5 describes an "elongated manifold" in the herbal section and flags the pharmaceutical-inside-herbal lobe geometry, but these are visual descriptions of a UMAP projection, not substantive claims about an intrinsic manifold. The quantitative cluster claim is carried by the classifier, not by UMAP. Good. **Not triggered**, but I want to flag a softer version of this criterion below under revision asks — a null-data control on matched marginals would be a trivially cheap addition that would turn an implicit "not-triggered" into a bulletproof "empirically tested."
2. *UMAP hyperparameter sweep absent.* Only a single (n_neighbors, min_dist) setting is reported, and the 10-seed Procrustes is on **seeds at fixed hyperparameters**, not on the (n_neighbors, min_dist) grid. This is the one place I want to push. I do not think the claim depends on UMAP hyperparameters because the quantitative evidence is supervised classification in the 16-d space directly, but a reader who is convinced by Figure 4 rather than Table 1 will reasonably ask whether the pharmaceutical-inside-herbal lobe, the herbal internal structure, and the five-cluster separation all persist under (n_neighbors ∈ {5, 15, 30, 50}, min_dist ∈ {0.0, 0.1, 0.5}). This is a revision-grade gap. **Soft trigger**; fixable by a supplementary figure.
3. *Principal-curve goodness-of-fit not reported.* The paper does not fit principal curves. The "elongated manifold" language in §5.5 point 1 is the one place where I would press. If the authors want to describe the herbal section as having internal structure they should either (a) drop the word "manifold" or (b) fit a principal curve and report fraction-of-variance captured and mean perpendicular distance. Since the Currier-hand comparison is explicitly deferred to future work, option (a) is fine with me — soften the language from "elongated manifold" to "elongated distribution" or "visible internal variation" and the problem goes away. **Not triggered by evidence**, triggered only by wording.
4. *Matched-capacity baseline missing.* This is the kill criterion I expected to fire and was surprised not to. The 16-d archetype lens is, from a DR perspective, a projection from 768-d CLIP space down to 16-d via 16 fixed text-anchor directions. I expected no matched-capacity baseline. The paper provides one in §5.3.1: the raw 768-d embedding reaches 92.4%, and the 16-d lens reaches 90.4%. This tells me the projection is not destroying much discriminative information. It does not, however, tell me whether **PCA-to-16** on the 768-d embedding matches the lens's 90.4%. That is the matched-capacity baseline I want. If PCA-to-16 also reaches ≈90%, the lens's contribution is interpretability, not dimensionality-reduction quality. If PCA-to-16 drops to, say, 75%, then the text-anchored lens is doing real DR work beyond generic variance-preservation. Either answer is fine; the number is not in the paper and I want it.
5. *Density confounds.* The classes are heavily imbalanced (herbal 118 vs astronomical 12). UMAP's density-distortion behaviour (Chari & Pachter 2023; Lause, Berens, Kobak 2024) is relevant. The authors use LOOCV + Wilson CIs for the quantitative claims, which is the right defence; the UMAP in Figure 4 is a visualisation, not evidence. No kill, but a one-line acknowledgement of UMAP density distortion in §5.5 would foreclose a reviewer comment.

**Evaluation-rubric pass.** Null data control (no; would be cheap). UMAP hyperparameter sweep (partial — seed only, not grid). Principal-curve GOF (N/A if "manifold" is softened). Distinguishes projection-head effects from raw DR (yes, via §5.3.1, but matched-capacity PCA-to-16 missing). Alternative DR shown (PCA shown in Fig 4c; no t-SNE or PaCMAP, which I would recommend as a robustness check).

**Intellectual-commitment comments.** The paper's own framing in §5.5 is epistemically humble: "We do not attempt to resolve the hand/style correlation in this paper; we only report that the system has picked up *some* kind of internal structure." This is the correct tone. The PCA-alongside-UMAP in Figure 4 is the single most important methodological concession to my criticism tradition in this paper, and it is done correctly — a deterministic linear projection as a sanity check against the non-deterministic non-linear one. If more papers did this, half my review load would disappear.

**JOCCH-specific concerns.** JOCCH's reviewer pool will include CV/ML people who know UMAP. The paper's current treatment is defensible but not bulletproof. The gap between "defensible" and "bulletproof" at JOCCH is:

- Add the (n_neighbors, min_dist) grid as a supplementary figure.
- Add a null-data control (Gaussian-shuffle of the 16-d profile matrix at matched per-dimension mean/var, rerun UMAP, show it produces no clustering) — one paragraph and one supplementary figure.
- Add PCA-to-16 as a matched-capacity baseline in the §5.3.3 table. This is a one-line sklearn addition.
- Soften "manifold" in §5.5 point 1 to "elongated distribution" unless authors want to fit a principal curve.

None of these are evidence-absent-and-cannot-be-supplied. They are revisions I would ask any JOCCH submission for.

**Framing note from the JOCCH vantage.** The dossier's hot-button #1 is "no ablations — VLM backbone, 16-dim archetype lens, and cross-corpus comparison each need contribution-isolating controls." The paper has the backbone ablation (raw 768-d, §5.3.1), the lens ablation (archaeology + cryptological lens specificity, §5.4), and the cross-corpus control (§5.9). The one missing ablation from *my* vantage that also matters to JOCCH is the PCA-to-K matched-capacity baseline for the lens. Add it. This is a single number.

**Specific revision requests.**

- Add a null-data control in §5.5: shuffle each of the 16 dimensions independently to break cross-dim covariance while preserving marginals; rerun UMAP and classifier; confirm classifier drops to ≈59.9% and UMAP blobs do not separate. One paragraph, one supplementary figure.
- Add a (n_neighbors, min_dist) grid in a supplementary figure. 3 × 3 = 9 panels; all the claimed cluster structure should survive the grid or the UMAP-based narrative in §5.5 needs to be weakened.
- Add PCA-to-16 as a fourth row in the §5.3.3 comparison table. Matched-capacity baseline is the one ablation not currently in the paper.
- Soften "elongated manifold" to "elongated distribution" in §5.5 unless a principal-curve GOF is added.
- Add a one-line density-distortion acknowledgement in §5.5 citing the UMAP-criticism literature.

**Verdict rationale.** No kill criterion fires on the substantive claims, which are carried by supervised classification, not by UMAP. The UMAP figure itself is used defensibly (PCA sanity check, seed-ensemble, claim-carried-by-classifier). The gaps are (a) matched-capacity PCA baseline, (b) hyperparameter grid, (c) null-data control, (d) one wording softening. All fixable in a revision. This is not an accept — the PCA-to-16 baseline is a gap a JOCCH reviewer will raise — but it is well short of a reject.

## Persona 3 — probing_methodology

### Review

**Summary assessment.** The 16-d archetype lens is, in my terminology, a probe — a set of 16 linear cosine-similarity directions (one per text-anchor) applied to a frozen CLIP embedding, used to make claims about what the frozen model encodes about illustrated medieval codex content. Whether the paper's quantitative claims are *about the model* (CLIP "encodes" medieval-codex semantics) or *about the manuscript* (the Voynich imagery "contains" thematic structure recoverable through this probe) is the central question a probing-methodology reviewer has to adjudicate. The paper mostly frames its claims correctly — the abstract, §1, and §6 all use "machine-measurable," "computationally accessible," "discriminates" rather than "the model understands" or "CLIP knows." This is the right framing under Hewitt & Liang-style probing discipline. I will flag a couple of places where the framing slips and a couple of places where standard probing-methodology controls are missing or implicit.

**Kill criteria — walkthrough.**

1. *Probe result presented as model-property without probe-capacity calibration.* The main quantitative result (90.4% LOOCV) is carried by a multinomial logistic regression with 16-d inputs and 5 outputs — a linear probe of known capacity. The paper does not report a full Hewitt & Liang control-task null (a random-mapping control task with the same probe). It does report: (i) a permutation-label null, which I consider a different control (it tests feature-to-label information, not probe capacity); (ii) two alternative 16-d lenses (archaeology, cryptological) in §5.4 which serve as probe-reselection controls showing the result is not lens-specific; (iii) a handcrafted 6-d layout probe (§5.3.2, 72.1%) showing what a lower-capacity probe achieves on the same labels. The combination of (ii) and (iii) is a partial probe-capacity calibration, but it is not the classical Hewitt & Liang random-mapping control. **Soft trigger**; see revision asks.
2. *No control-task / random-mapping null reported.* No. This is the missing canonical control. The test would be: replace the 16-d archetype lens with 16 random text prompts (same length distribution, same model, same cosine-similarity probe), rerun the classifier under the same LOOCV protocol. If that random-prompt probe recovers sections at 80%, then the lens's "interpretability" story is undermined — the probe was going to find *something* no matter what the prompts said. If the random-prompt probe falls to, say, 65%, then the semantic content of the prompts is doing real work. This is one of the most methodologically informative controls the paper could add, and it is not there. **Triggered**, but fixable in a revision with a single supplementary experiment.
3. *Cross-modal cosine similarity used without addressing modality-gap artifact risk.* Cross-modal cosine similarity between CLIP image embeddings and CLIP text embeddings is known to sit in a narrow band distorted by the modality gap (Liang et al. 2022, *Mind the Gap*). The paper's §3.1 describes "cosine similarity between the two embeddings" as the profile entry; §3.2 acknowledges unspecified "cosine-similarity normalisation and temperature scaling" is applied but declines to disclose it. This is where the patent-disclosure curtain intersects my methodology concerns. I cannot independently audit whether the modality-gap correction is appropriate. A reviewer cannot verify that the 16-d profile values are not artefacts of a specific normalisation regime. **Flagged as a minor trigger**; the paper's overall disclosure posture under the non-disclosure boundary (§7, Appendix B) is explicit about this, but a reader with my concerns needs a slightly louder acknowledgement that the modality-gap is a known risk and that the normalisation choice affects it.
4. *Researcher-degrees-of-freedom in probe construction unacknowledged.* §3.1 point 2 and §6.6 first bullet acknowledge that the 16 dimensions were authored by the authors with medieval-codex content in mind but without Voynich images or labels in the tuning loop. This is good — a rare explicit acknowledgement. It does *not* say how many revisions the dimension list went through, whether dimensions that performed poorly in pilot analyses were silently dropped, or whether the descriptor wording was iterated. The lens-specificity control in §5.4 addresses the stronger form of this concern (alternative lenses also work), but the weaker "garden of forking paths on the voynich lens itself" concern is not addressed. **Minor trigger**; a one-paragraph provenance note on lens authorship would close it.

**Evaluation-rubric pass.** Probe identified (yes — linear MLR on 16-d cosine similarities). Probe capacity characterised (partially — via comparison with 6-d and 768-d probes, not via Hewitt & Liang random-mapping null). Distinguishes model from probe (yes, implicitly, via lens-specificity control). Probe revision history (no, not documented). Modality gap (not addressed explicitly).

**Intellectual-commitment comments.** Probes are measurement instruments; they should be calibrated first and used second. The 16-d archetype lens is *uncalibrated* in the Hewitt-Liang sense — we are told what it measures, we are told that alternative lenses produce similar results, we are told that lower-capacity probes (6-d layout) produce weaker results, and we are told that the raw 768-d embedding produces slightly stronger results. What we are *not* told is: if you replace the content of the dimension descriptors with lorem-ipsum text but keep the same number of dimensions and the same probe architecture, what does the probe recover? This is the calibration step. Without it the paper's claim is "this probe with this content recovers sections at 90.4%"; with it the claim strengthens to "this probe's semantic content — not its capacity — is doing the work." The strengthened claim is the one the paper wants to make. The calibration is a one-experiment revision ask.

**JOCCH-specific concerns.** JOCCH's hot-button list includes "missing baselines: non-lens CLIP-only classifier, shuffled-label control, text-channel head-to-head." The paper ships all three: §5.3.1 (non-lens CLIP-only = raw 768-d, 92.4%), §5.3 (label-permutation 1000 shuffles, p≈1e-3), §5.10 (text-channel head-to-head, 92.3% = 92.3%). From the JOCCH dossier's published bar the paper clears the baseline requirement. The gap I am adding — random-prompt probe — is *my* discipline's canonical null, not JOCCH's. I mark it as a revision ask rather than a kill criterion because it is an NLP-probing-methodology convention, not a universal CV convention, and JOCCH reviewers may or may not escalate the ask. I expect they will, because the modality-gap-aware subset of JOCCH's pool is non-trivial in 2026.

Separately: the paper's §6.4 claim that "the archetype lens's contribution is not classification accuracy but legibility of a specific kind that a humanities audience needs" is a probing-methodology claim I think is *correct* but that the paper has not quite proved. The correct proof would be: (a) raw 768-d is most accurate, (b) 16-d archetype lens loses 2 accuracy points but gains interpretability, (c) 16-d random-prompt probe loses X accuracy points relative to both, (d) 6-d layout is the floor. The paper has (a), (b), and (d). Adding (c) closes the argument.

**Framing-slip checks.** I searched for "understand," "know," "interpret" used of the model. The paper is disciplined: "discriminate" is the dominant verb; "recover" is used of the classifier; "encode" is used narrowly in "the illustrations encode structured thematic content," which is a claim about the manuscript, not the model. §5.1 says "the system has recovered the manuscript's sections in a way that a Voynich scholar would describe as *right for the right reason*." This is a borderline anthropomorphism — "right for the right reason" is ambiguous between "the classifier's feature-weights match scholarly expectation" (fine) and "the classifier *knows* what the sections are about" (not fine). I would ask for this to be tightened to the former. The rest of the framing is clean.

**Specific revision requests.**

- Add a random-prompt probe as a supplementary control. 16 semantically uninformative prompts of matched length; same CLIP encoder; same probe architecture; report LOOCV accuracy. This is the Hewitt-Liang control-task equivalent for your setting.
- Add a one-paragraph lens-authorship provenance note in §3.1 or §6.6: how many revisions; whether any dimensions were dropped mid-design; what the final set of 16 was selected against. Pre-register the next lens for a follow-up paper.
- Add a modality-gap acknowledgement in §3.1 or §6.6 citing Liang et al. 2022 and stating that the non-disclosed normalisation is designed to mitigate it. This does not require disclosing the normalisation; it requires acknowledging the risk.
- Tighten "right for the right reason" in §5.1 to avoid the anthropomorphism-adjacent reading. "The classifier's feature weights align with dimensions that scholarly readers would predict" is the defensible restatement.
- Consider re-framing the "probe" language throughout. Currently the paper uses "lens"; for a JOCCH audience including ML-probing readers, "lens (a 16-dimension linear probe over CLIP-text-anchor cosine similarities)" in §3.1 would situate the method inside the probing-methodology literature and earn useful credibility.

**Verdict rationale.** The probe-capacity calibration is partial, not absent. The lens-specificity control (§5.4) does most of the work the Hewitt-Liang null would do, just from a different angle. The framing is mostly disciplined. The modality-gap issue is real but modest given the disclosure boundary. None of these rise to a kill; all are fixable in a revision.
